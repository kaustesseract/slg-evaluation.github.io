Automated Sign Language Generator Evaluation, Monash University

Project Description

This research evaluates automated sign language generation systems. These systems create sign language videos or 3D signing avatars from text or other input.

Computer-based scores do not always show whether generated signs are easy to understand, natural to watch, or clear in meaning. For this reason, feedback from people who are proficient in sign language is important.

  1. This study is not judging your capability. It is judging the sign language generation system.
  2. You will not be told which video condition or generation method you are viewing during the evaluation.
  3. The project information explains the acknowledgement statement, what we are doing in this study, brief information about the project, and how implied consent works.

What You Will Do

  • Watch short sign language videos.
  • Answer questions about recognition, naturalness, clarity, and intended meaning.
  • Complete a short screening task if required.
  • Use media controls such as pause, replay, or playback speed where available.
Participation is voluntary. You may stop before submitting your responses, and you may skip any question that you do not want to answer.

Project Information Includes

  • An acknowledgement statement confirming that you have read and understood the study information.
  • A clear explanation of what the research team is doing in this study.
  • Brief information about the project purpose, task, risks, benefits, data use, and contact details.
  • An explanation that consent is implied when you submit your completed online survey responses.

Who Can Participate

Participants should be adults aged 18 years or over who are proficient in American Sign Language, German Sign Language, or Chinese Sign Language.

Data and Privacy

The webpage may record basic interaction information related to the task, such as replay, pause, playback speed, and approximate time spent on video questions. It will not use webcam recording, screen recording, audio recording, or keystroke logging.
